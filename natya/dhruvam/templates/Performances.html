Aigiri Nandini

Aigiri Nandini is a powerful and popular Sanskrit hymn, also known as Mahishasura Mardini Stotram. It is a devotional song dedicated to Goddess Durga or Chamunda, who is revered as the fierce form of the Divine Mother. The hymn praises her valor, beauty, strength, and her role as the slayer of the demon Mahishasura

Bharathanatya Varnam

Varnam is a pivotal component of a Bharatanatyam performance, often considered the most elaborate and expressive part of a recital. It serves as a bridge between the more abstract elements of dance and the expressive, emotive storytelling that Bharatanatyam is known for.

Maha Ganapathi

Maha Ganapathi is a popular and revered form of Lord Ganesha, the elephant-headed Hindu deity who is widely worshipped as the remover of obstacles, the god of beginnings, and the patron of arts and learning.

Padi Pattu

Padi Pattu songs are dedicated to Lord Ayyappa and performed in worship contexts like the Sabarimala pilgrimage. Dance sometimes accompanies these sacred songs to enhance the devotional atmosphere.

Mohiniyattam Padam

A Padam highlights the emotional and dramatic essence of the dance, where the focus is on Abhinaya (expression), dealing with themes like love, longing, devotion, and divine relationships.

Krishna Shabdam

Krishna Shabdam is a devotional composition, particularly in Bharatanatyam and Mohiniyattam, praising Lord Krishna, combining both Nritta (pure dance) and Abhinaya (expression).
